# Compliance Rules — California Commercial Leases

## Late Fee Caps (California Civil Code §1671)
- Late fees must be a reasonable estimate of damages.
- Typically capped at 5% of the monthly rent amount.
- Percentage-based fees above 5% are presumptively unreasonable.

## Security Deposit Limits
- For unfurnished commercial properties: no statutory cap in most cases,
  but industry standard is 1-2 months' rent.
- Deposits exceeding 2 months' rent should be flagged for review.

## Grace Period Requirements
- Minimum 3-day grace period before late fees can be assessed.
- Some municipalities require 5-10 day grace periods.

## Rent Escalation Disclosure
- Any rent escalation clause must clearly state:
  - The escalation rate or formula
  - The frequency of adjustments
  - Any caps or ceilings on increases
- Missing escalation terms should be flagged.

## Mandatory Disclosures (California)
- Lead-based paint disclosure (pre-1978 buildings)
- Earthquake disclosure (within seismic hazard zones)
- Mold disclosure
- Utility submetering disclosure (if applicable)
- One-to-one floor plan (if applicable)
